## TCP Three-Way Handshake & Scanning

TCP communication signals during port scanning: **SYN**, **SYN-ACK**, and **RST**.

---

## TCP vs UDP Scanning

| Aspect | TCP (Connection-Oriented) | UDP (Connectionless) |
|--------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| Analogy | Phone call — ring, pick up, hello | Throwing a paper plane — toss and hope |
| Handshake | Formal SYN → SYN-ACK → ACK | No handshake |
| Clarity | Clear if port is open/closed | Harder to determine state |

---

## Port Status Responses

| Port Status | TCP Scan Response | UDP Scan Response |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Open | SYN-ACK | Data returned, OR no response at all |
| Closed | RST (Reset) | ICMP Port Unreachable |
| Filtered | No response (firewall) | No response — Nmap gets confused |

---

## Speed and Reliability

| Protocol | Characteristic     |
| -------- | ------------------ |
| TCP      | Fast and reliable  |
| UDP      | Slow and ambiguous |
