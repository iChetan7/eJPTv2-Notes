## Cyber Security Fundamentals

### I. What is Cyber Security?

Computer Security, Cyber Security, or Information Technology Security is the protection of computer systems and networks from information disclosure, theft, or damage to their hardware, software, or electronic data.

---

### II. What We Are Securing

| Asset Type | Examples |
|------------|----------|
| Personal Identification Information | SSN, passport, driver's license |
| Healthcare Information | Medical records, prescriptions |
| Financial Data | Bank accounts, credit cards, transactions |
| Intellectual Property | Patents, copyrights, trade secrets |
| Business Secrets | Proprietary formulas, strategies |
| Business Operations | Supply chain, customer databases |

---

### III. Securing From Whom

| Threat Actor | Motivation |
|--------------|------------|
| Criminals | Financial gain, data theft |
| Competitors | Industrial espionage, market advantage |
| Insider Threats | Disgruntlement, negligence, coercion |
| Malicious Actors | Hacktivism, sabotage, nation-state objectives |

---

## CIA Triad

The foundational balance of information security:

| Principle | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| Confidentiality | Ensure information is accessible only to authorized parties |
| Integrity | Maintain accuracy and trustworthiness of data |
| Availability | Guarantee reliable access to information when needed |
