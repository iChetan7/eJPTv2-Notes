## What is Social Engineering?

**Human manipulation** — the art of hacking people rather than systems. The goal is either to **gain access** to systems/networks or to **gather information** from targets by exploiting human psychology.

---

## Core Tactics & Techniques

| Tactic                    | Description                                                                  |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Impersonation**         | Pretending to be someone trusted (CEO, IT support, vendor)                   |
| **Pretexting**            | Creating a believable scenario/story to justify the interaction              |
| **Emotional Pull**        | Leveraging fear, urgency, pity, or excitement to bypass critical thinking    |
| **Free Stuff / Baiting**  | Offering something attractive to lure the victim (USB drops, fake downloads) |
| **Blackmail / Extortion** | Threatening exposure or harm unless demands are met                          |
| **Quid Pro Quo**          | "You give a little, you get a little" — offering help in exchange for info   |
| **Romance Scams**         | Building fake relationships to extract money or data                         |

---

## Types of Phishing Attacks

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Phishing** | Mass email campaign to steal credentials or deliver malware |
| **Spear Phishing** | Targeted phishing against specific individuals or organizations |
| **Whaling** | Spear phishing aimed at high-value targets (C-suite, wealthy individuals) |
| **Smishing** | Phishing via SMS/text messages |
| **Vishing** | Phishing via phone calls (voice + phishing) |
| **Pharming** | Redirecting traffic to fake websites that look legitimate |
| **Business Email Compromise (BEC)** | Hijacking or spoofing legitimate business emails for fraud |

---

## Other Social Engineering Methods

| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **Watering Hole** | Compromising a website frequented by targets to infect them |
| **Baiting** | Leaving infected USB drives ("rubber duckies") in public places |
| **Physical Access** | Tailgating, impersonating service workers, or using props (ladders, blue folders) to bypass security |

---

## FBI 2021 Crime Statistics

| Statistic | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Most reported attacks** | Phishing, vishing, smishing, pharming — **323,000+ complaints** |
| **Highest financial losses** | Business Email Compromise (BEC), confidence/romance fraud |
| **Trend** | Complaints and losses increasing year-over-year for past 5 years |
| **Malware vs. SE** | Social engineering complaints **far outweigh** malware, ransomware, and scareware combined |

## Why People Fall For It

| Factor | Example |
|---|---|
| **Trust exploitation** | "The commander asked me to send you this file" |
| **Visual deception** | Fake login pages indistinguishable from real ones (Apple Store examples) |
| **Urgency/Fear** | "Your account will be suspended — click now" |
| **Authority** | Fake CEO email requesting gift cards |
| **Familiarity** | Spoofed supplier email with slight typo (`supplierss@...`) |

---

## Physical Social Engineering Examples

| Technique | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Tailgating** | Following employees into building during rush hour |
| **Prop-based** | Carrying a ladder or blue folder = instant "belonging" |
| **Impersonation** | Fake cable guy, plumber, electrician with inside info |
| **USB Baiting** | Dropping infected USBs in parking lots — auto-runs payload when plugged in |

> **Real-world impact**: US military banned all USBs after successful baiting attacks in early 2010s.

---

## Defenses Against Social Engineering

| Layer | Defense |
|---|---|
| **User Awareness & Training** | #1 prescribed method — teach users to verify, not click, check URLs |
| **Security Policies** | Email filtering, link scanning, attachment sandboxing |
| **Defense in Depth** | Zero trust, network segmentation, least privilege |
| **Hardware/Software Controls** | Proxies, firewalls, email security gateways (e.g., IRONSCALES) |

## Key Takeaways

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Core principle** | People want to trust — attackers exploit this |
| **Most effective attack vector** | Phishing (by volume) and BEC (by financial loss) |
| **Physical access** | Often bypasses all technical defenses |
| **Pen testing relevance** | Social engineering is a legitimate penetration testing technique |
| **Prevention** | User training + technical controls + defense in depth |
